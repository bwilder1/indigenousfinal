Firsting & Lasting



What It Is

Firsting & Lasting is a concept created by Jean M. O'Brien in her book Firsting and Lasting. It was created to show how colonizers came to America and created their own history by considering themselves to be the first people in there. Lasting then shows that Native Americans are slowly being assimilated into western culture, deeming the last full blooded Native American as extinct, meaning there are no "real" Native Americans left.

Town

Osage Beach was established in 1886 in Missouri. In 1931, the Bagnell Dam was completed which lead to the creation of the Lake of the Ozarks. By 1959 the town was incorporated and there are around 4,900 residents living there today. The town is named after the Osage Tribe which had members that resided in that area before settlers came. Currently, over 90% of the population is white, and under 1% (0.84%) are Native American.

School of the Osage

The School of the Osage consists of schools ranging from Pre-K level to high school. The home page of their website says "School of the Osage: Home of the Indians", yet it's hard to understand exactly why it's the home of the Indians, given only 0.3% of the school is Native American and 86.8% are white. Playing into the role of Firsting and Lasting, Osage high school is using the "Indian" to claim rights over the land, making it seem like this WAS the home to Native Americans and that there are no more left. Throughout the website there are kids wearing shirts with slogans saying "roll tribe roll" and "Indians". The ignorance of this is extremely dangerous in an educational setting, as kids will eventually view Native Americans as a historic population rather than one that continues to fight against injustices.


School Logo

The logo for the School of the Osage is a classic settler interpretation of the head of a Native American. Although it doesn't have too much detail, the headdress full of feathers is a stereotypical depiction of Native Americans. Previously in their gym, they had an undeniably racist image depicting Native Americans plastered on the walls (see article below). The use of this imagery in contradiction to them being the "Home of the Indians", goes to show their everlasting relationship with Firsting and Lasting.
